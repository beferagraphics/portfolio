The Objective

Rodeo Networks came to me in 2015 looking for a designer who could work remotely and provide quick and efficient design work with little artistic direction. Most of what I designed was marketed to users who knew the ins and outs of terminology like "WISP" and "IPTV". Even so, they wanted to come across as a fun but reliable brand that prides themselves as being a step above the competition in both product quality and customer service.

The first project I did for Rodeo was a tri-fold brochure detailing their services and bundles. During this first foray into the brand's image, I was challenged with the task of vectorizing some commissioned illustrations in order to make them large enough to print.

Shortly after, Rodeo needed a clean but engaging double-sided flyer design for a show they were attending to advertise their product to other TV providers and their customers. I was able to compile a large amount of information into a small space while maintaining readability as well as keeping the design cohesive with the brand image.

Most recently, I created a series of ads for Rodeo's presence at WISPAmerica 2018. Their mission for this particular show was to advertise that RodeoTV is on a level of its own with tons of unique features that potential future customers can try out at their show booth, with an emphasis on IPTV.

The Results

Rodeo's professional and upbeat designs stand out among the bland corporate aesthetic of many of their competitors. Since I began work for them, the company has blossomed into not just a booming local service but a country-wide enterprise with customers in several major cities from Seattle to Miami.
